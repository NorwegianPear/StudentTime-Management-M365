❓ What Problem Does This Solve?

Many schools using Microsoft 365 face a common challenge: students can log in to their school accounts at any time of day or night — evenings, weekends, holidays. This leads to:

⚠ Students accessing school services outside school hours — gaming platforms, social apps, or content not suitable for unsupervised use.
⚠ No automatic enforcement — teachers and IT staff have to manually intervene when issues arise.
⚠ Microsoft 365 has no built-in schedule feature — there is no native "disable logins after 16:00" option in the platform.

Our solution fills this gap by using Azure Automation to automatically enable and disable student accounts on a schedule you define — for example, Monday–Friday 07:50–16:05, with no access on weekends or holidays.

⚙ How Does It Work? (Plain Language)

The solution runs entirely in Microsoft's own cloud (Azure). Here is what happens every school day, automatically — without anyone needing to do anything:

1 07:50 — Azure Automation wakes up
A scheduled job in Azure runs automatically. It connects to your Microsoft 365 tenant using a secure registered app — no passwords stored in scripts, fully audited.
2 It reads your student group
The automation looks up a security group in your Microsoft 365 tenant — e.g., "Students - Class 2025". Every member of that group is managed by the schedule. Teachers are never in this group.
3 Student accounts are enabled 🔓
Each student account is set to "enabled" via the Microsoft Graph API. Students can now sign in to Teams, OneDrive, school computers — everything works as normal.
4 16:05 — Student accounts are disabled 🔒
A second scheduled job runs. All student accounts are set to "disabled". Active sessions are revoked immediately. Students are signed out and cannot sign back in until the next morning.
5 Everything is logged 📊
Every enable and disable action is written to Azure Log Analytics. You can see a full audit trail of which accounts were changed and when. Alerts can be configured if a job fails.
💡 Real-life example — What a student experiences:

Emma is in 7th grade. On Monday morning at 07:55 she can log in to her school computer and Teams — everything works normally. At 16:05 she is automatically signed out and cannot log back in. If she is in the middle of writing a document, it has already auto-saved to OneDrive — no work is lost. On Saturday she tries to log in from home — access is denied. On Monday morning everything works again automatically, without IT staff doing anything.

Teacher and staff accounts are never affected — only students in the designated group are managed by the schedule.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will teacher and staff accounts be affected by the schedule?

No. Only students who are members of the designated security group (e.g., "Students - All") are controlled by the schedule. Teacher, admin, and IT staff accounts are never touched — they continue to work at all times.
Q: What happens if a student is writing a document at 16:05 when accounts are locked?

Microsoft 365 apps (Word, Teams, OneDrive) save automatically and continuously. When the session is revoked, the student's work is already saved to the cloud. They will not lose anything. The account disable is a login block, not a data deletion — all their files remain exactly as they were.
Q: A student has an exam tomorrow evening — can we give them extended access?

Yes. With Option 1, an IT admin runs a PowerShell script to temporarily re-enable specific students or change the schedule for that day. With Option 2 (Portal), any authorised staff member — teacher, admin, principal — can do this in two clicks from their browser. No IT involvement required.
Q: Do we need to buy extra Microsoft 365 licenses?

No. The solution uses only Azure Automation and Entra ID features that are already available with a standard Microsoft 365 Education or Business subscription. No new licenses, no additional Microsoft software purchases.
Q: What does "nonprofit pricing" mean, and does our school qualify?

Microsoft offers significant discounts and free credits to registered nonprofit and educational organisations. If your school qualifies, you can receive ~37,450 NOK/year in free Azure credits and heavily discounted or free GitHub. We can help you check eligibility and complete the application — it typically takes 1–2 weeks and we handle most of the paperwork.
Q: Is this secure? What access does the solution need to our Microsoft 365 tenant?

Yes. The solution uses a dedicated Azure App Registration (a service account) with the minimum permissions required: read group members, and enable/disable user accounts. It cannot read emails, access files, or do anything outside those two operations. All activity is logged. No passwords are stored — the connection uses modern OAuth 2.0 certificate-based authentication.
Q: How long does the implementation take?

Typically 4–8 hours of active work, spread over approximately one week. This includes: setting up the Azure environment, configuring the automation rules, connecting to your Microsoft 365 tenant, pilot testing with a small group of students, validation, and full documentation handover to your IT team.
